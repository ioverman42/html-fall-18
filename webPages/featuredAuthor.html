Author of the Month

Patrick Rothfuss

Patrick Rothfuss always wanted to be fantasy author when he grew up. Now that his first novel is published it's generally agreed that he has achieved his dream. However, there is some debate as to whether or not he has, in fact, grown up. An avid Dungeons and Dragons fan, Rothfuss is also known for his love of board games and role playing games. He has guest starred multiple times on Wil Wheaton's Tabletop.

Native Wisconsinite, Rothfuss went to DeForest High School and UW Stevens Point before getting his masters at Washington State University. He has received the Quill Award for Sci-Fi/Fantasy/Horror in 2007 for his first book The Name of the Wind, and the David Gemmel Award for best fantasy novel in 2012 for the second book of The Kingkiller Chronicle series, Wise Man's Fear.

Additionally, Rothfuss has published The Slow Regard of Silent Things—which is a companion tale to The Kingkiller Chronicle series—and two novella stories of his have been published in anthologies: "How Old Holly Came To Be", in Unfettered, and The Lightning Tree in Rogues.

Reviews

"I was reminded of Ursula LeGuin, George R. R. Martin, and J. R. R. Tolkien, but never felt that Rothfuss was imitating anyone. Like the writers he clearly admires, he's an old-fashioned storyteller working with traditional elements, but his voice is his own. I haven't been so gripped by a new fantasy series in years. It's certain to become a classic."
-The London Times

"[The Wise Man's Fear] was worth the wait. I gulped it down in a day, staying up almost to dawn reading, and I am already itching for the next one. He's bloody good, this Rothfuss guy."
-George R. R. Martin,
author of A Game of Thrones

"This is a magnificent book, a really fine story, highly readable and engrossing. I compliment young Pat. His first novel is a great one. Wow!"
-Anne McCaffrey
